911 Call Guide
Medical Emergencies
• Choking: Ask if they can speak or cough. If not, instruct on back blows and abdominal thrusts.
• Heart Attack: Have them sit down, loosen tight clothing. Ask if they have medication.
• Seizure: Clear area of hard objects. Do not restrain. Time the seizure.
• Bleeding: Apply direct pressure with clean cloth. Elevate injury if possible.
• Allergic Reaction: Ask if they have an epinephrine auto-injector. Help them use it if available.
Violent Situations
• Active Shooter: Instruct to Run, Hide, Fight. Find cover, silence phones.
• Domestic Violence: Keep caller calm. Ask if safe to talk. Get location and suspect description.
• Kidnapping: Get vehicle description, direction of travel, last known location.
• Hostage Situation: Keep caller hidden if possible. Gather information quietly.
• Fight in Progress: Get number of people involved, weapons visible, location specifics.
Fire Emergencies
• Structure Fire: Confirm everyone is out. Do not re-enter building.
• Vehicle Fire: Move at least 100 feet away. Warn about potential explosion.
• Wildfire: Evacuate immediately. Close all windows and doors before leaving.
• Electrical Fire: Do not use water. Use Class C fire extinguisher if available.
Traffic Incidents
• Major Collision: Check for injuries, fuel leaks, hazardous materials.
• Vehicle in Water: Guide to escape through windows. Vehicle will float momentarily.
• Rollover: Do not move injured persons unless in immediate danger.
• Pedestrian Struck: Secure scene. Do not move victim. Control traffic if safe.
Psychological Crises
• Suicide Threat: Keep caller talking. Remove means of self-harm if possible.
• Mental Health Crisis: Speak calmly. Avoid sudden movements. Create safe space.
• Panic Attack: Guide through deep breathing. Focus on present moment.
